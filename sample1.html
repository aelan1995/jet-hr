STARTUP STAGE


PRESENTATION

Nindo operates in the field of ICT. Its goal is to provide a service to improve the performance of systems, such as energy efficient buildings, via data-driven modeling and automation algorithms developed integrating competencies from Artificial Intelligence and Control theory.

PRODUCT/SERVICE



The service to be provided is based on the remote exchange of data between the user and a server system, which can be either a local server or a cloud service such as, for example, Amazon Web Server (AWS). The main idea is to acquire system data on a central server, such as temperature, humidity, CO2, energy, etc., of a building, and use them to create mathematical models, using combined techniques of Machine Learning and Identification Theory, which describe the dynamics of the system itself, in order to be able to make predictions, for example, of the temperature trend and energy consumption of the building. Subsequently, always on the local server, predictive control algorithms will be also implemented to provide, starting from the derived models, control inputs to ensure that climate control is operated on the building is capable of guaranteeing thermal comfort for the occupants and at the same time save energy. The data resulting from these processings, being either prediction only or also control, are then sent by the central server to the user according to his needs, i.e. knowledge of the consumption of the building over a predictive horizon or climate control. The same service can be used in a similar way also in other applications, as for example the control of telecommunications networks, structural monitoring, genomic classification, and others similar to the purposes of the start-up. The algorithms to be implemented will be based on the results obtained during the scientific research carried out by the proponents in recent years, which led to the publication of several international articles, both in prestigious journals and international conferences, and a patent.

BUSINESS MODEL AND SOURCE OF PROFITS

The latest IEA report from 2021 shows that buildings are responsible for 30% of global final energy consumption and 27% of total emissions from the energy sector, of which 8% is made up of direct emissions in buildings and 19% from indirect emissions from the production of electricity and heat used in the same. These numbers represent an increase of almost 20% compared to the situation of 2010, in particular going from a building energy consumption from 115EJ in 2010 to 135EJ in 2021. One of the main concerns is given by the forecasts for 2050, in which these numbers are destined to grow dramatically. Further aggravating the situation is the spike in energy costs. For this reason, the use of more efficient and renewable energy technologies in buildings is accelerating significantly, with the aim of achieving the "Net Zero Emission" goal by 2050. For this goal, the next decade will be crucial to implement the necessary measures so that all new buildings and 20% of the existing building stock are "zero-carbon-ready" as early as 2030 [1]. To do this, of course, it is not enough to rely solely on renewable energy sources, but it is necessary to reduce energy consumption itself, both for environmental and economic reasons. In such a scenario, numerous companies are springing up around the world in this context. Wanting to consider only the national market, according to the ISTAT data of the last census available, i.e. that of 2011, the buildings currently present in Italy are around 14.5 million, of which around 85% are residential buildings and more specifically around 52% are single houses [2]. In this context, the goal is to exploit the technology now present in existing or new buildings in order to carry out monitoring and automation for energy efficiency. Considering the size of the market and the numbers involved, many companies are springing up even just in the sale of hardware technologies suitable for the purpose, such as Netatmo, Shelly, Sonoff, and others, which create easily installable remote monitoring and automation devices, and therefore suitable for small buildings, typically residential. However, all these companies do not provide services for the implementation of methodologies for optimizing energy efficiency. Some of them, such as Netatmo for example, limit themselves to providing basic methodologies for climate control, without pretensions of energy optimization, but only of reduction of consumption. The main reason is that this type of activity requires specific skills that are difficult to find, as they are closely linked to scientific research. This startup was born in this context, or in offering a monitoring, prediction and automation service for optimal energy efficiency by exploiting advanced methodologies available in the scientific panorama and partly developed in the research activity of the proponents. What has been said up to this point concerns the market relating to energy efficient buildings, but it can be scaled with other numbers also with reference to other application fields, as for example the control of telecommunications networks, structural monitoring, genomic classification, and others similar to the purposes of the start-up. COMPETITORS

In recent years, the use of Artificial Intelligence in all those fields of applications that concern the improvement of life has undergone a dizzying growth, from energy efficiency to smart cities, from autonomous driving to smart entrepreneurship. In this context there are many companies that have established themselves, and startups that are emerging, which offer cloud services for the remote management for different problems. Many of these services are offered by large established companies, such as Amazon Web Services, IBM Watson, Meta Platforms, others have instead been developed by startups which were then acquired by large companies, such as the case of DeepMind, which was acquired by Google . Then there are companies that, although born less than 20 years ago, have grown dramatically over time, establishing themselves as a reference in the sector, such as Cloudera, Salesforce, SenseTime, Dataiku, H2O.ai, and the nowadays leaders C3.ai and OpenAI [1 ]. The main problem of these large platforms is that some of them "limit themselves" to offering pre-set services for system management, therefore not customizable ad-hoc according to customer needs, unless obviously receiving large proposals, others instead offer cloud services and equipment which, however, must then be programmed and managed by third parties. For this reason, several startups have been born in the last decade to meet these problems, offering services dedicated to the specific needs of even the "smallest" customers. This innovative frontier is also booming in Italy, especially in the smart cities and smart buildings sector. The Internet of Things Observatory of the Milan Polytechnic, in collaboration with the Startup Intelligence Observatory, analyzed 307 startups, both Italian and international, operating in the sector, 212 of which received funding in the last 3 years [2]. In particular, 11 were selected to present their innovations, of which 6 operating in the Building Automation sector, from energy saving to structural monitoring, from indoor navigation to internal monitoring. At local level, in Abruzzo, such advanced realities do not seem to exist. There are companies in the area that offer energy saving services, which offer the possibility of automatically and autonomously managing the plants inside a building. However, this automatic management is limited to the possibility of switching lights and systems on and off based on pre-settings or based on the presence of people measured by detectors, but no services are offered for the effective optimization of consumption. Short description of the aspects of innovation that characterize the business enterprise

The activities of this startup represent the natural and necessary evolution of the technology transfer of the research activities of the proponents regarding the energy efficiency of buildings. In particular, the idea is to exploit the data that are collected inside a building, such as temperature, humidity, CO2, energy consumption, lighting, etc., to create mathematical models of the dynamics of the building by exploiting techniques that combine the use of Machine Learning and Identification methods. These models can then be used both to provide predictions on the behavior of the building dynamics, such as for example the prediction of room temperature and energy consumption, in order for example to provide cost predictions, and to set up predictive control algorithms to optimize the energy consumption of the building guaranteeing thermal or visual comfort for the occupants. The same modeling and control technique can also be applied in other application fields, such as for example the traffic control of telecommunications networks, in which the traffic models of a network can be generated starting from the historical traffic data collected during the provision of the standard service. Subsequently these models can be used to dynamically and optimally control the bandwidth allocation and priorities for the various services. These approaches can also be used for the only purpose of modeling and therefore prediction, not aimed at control. For example, in structural monitoring applications, dynamic models of the oscillation of structures can be derived from accelerometric data, and these models can be used to detect any damage inside structures, such as bridges or buildings. In the biological field, this modeling can be used to model certain genomic conditions, such as that linked to epilepsy, and to use classification algorithms to identify their typology. The main innovation consists in the fact that the modeling and automation of systems of such complexity with "classical" methods is currently prohibitive from the point of view of time, costs and knowledge, and the techniques that the startup proposes do overcome this gap. In particular, the methodologies that we intend to apply for our activities come from the scientific research carried out by the proponents in recent years at the University of L'Aquila.
